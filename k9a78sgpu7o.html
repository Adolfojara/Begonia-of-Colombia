Diagnosis

Begonia mamapachensis is morphologically similar to Begonia cornuta, Begonia lawrencei, and Begonia galeanoi,  but can be distinguished from them by having leaves lanceolate (vs. ovate or oblong), oblong (vs. ovate), and inflorescence a monochasium with few flowers (vs. dichasial with many flowers).

Stipules early caducous, 6.6−7.3 x 3.6−5.6 mm, lanceolate, glabrous, light green to hyaline, membranous, adaxially papillate, margin entire, apex acute to obtuse and aristate, ariste ca. 0.7 mm. Petioles 1.1–5.5 cm. Inflorescences dichasial cymes; bisexual or only staminate flowers, protrandrous; peduncle 3.1–4.6 mm long. Bracts caduceus, 4.4–7.1 × 2.6–3.7 mm, elliptic to lanceolate; apex acute; margin entire. Etymology

Refers to the name of the mountain to which this species is endemic, the Cerro Mamapacha in the Colombian department of Boyacá, shared by the municipalities of Garagoa and Chinavita.  

Habitat and distribution

Begonia mamapachensis is endemic to the mountains known as Cerro Mamapacha, in a range between 2100 and 2500 m. Plants of the species has been found growing in the understory of the cloud forest, usually along tracks and stream borders.

Proposed IUCN conservation status

Because the extent of occurrence (43000 km2) the species is critically endangered (CR), and according to the area of occupancy (28000 km2) it is endangered (EN). The region where the species grows has a moderate intervention of agriculture and pasture, and is not part of any national or regional protected area, however, there are private reserves in the zone of Cerro Mamapacha. The plant is cultivated now in the special collection for conservation (CEPAC) at the Bogotá Botanic Garden.

Habitat and distribution

Begonia pax es endémica de la región sur de la Serranía de los Yariguíes. La especie ha sido registrada principalmente en el sotobosque de bosque de niebla, creciendo en diferentes sustratos, incluidas las rocas cársticas que forman zonas cavernosas en esta región.
