Begonia antioquensis (A.DC.) Warb. Nat. Pflanzenfam. 3(6A): 146. A.DC. Casparya antioquiensis A.DC. Annales des Sciences Naturelles; Botanique, série 4 11: 116. 1859. TYPE: COLOMBIA. 1851-57. \textit{Triana s.n.} (holotype, P; isotype, K). 
Herb, 0.4 m. Branches pubescent or villous, stems and hairs reddish, hairs scale-like. \textbf{Stipules} sub-persistent, 6.8–8.1 mm long $\times$ 4.5–4.8 mm, ovate, pubescent, coriaceous, dark brown, obtuse, margin entire. Petiole 2.8–3.9 mm long., villous. \textbf{Leaves} mostly straight; 5.6–5.7 $\times$ 1.9–2 cm; oblogo-elliptic, or lanceolate; strongly asymmetryc; base rounded on one side, acute on the other side; apex acute; margin setose-serrate; above tuberculo-pilose; beneath tuberculo-pilose, or ferrugineo-tomentose on the veins; 5 veins pairs. \textbf{Infloresence} bisexual. \textbf{Male flowers} distal and female flowers basal; protandrous, dichasial, 1–2 times divided; peduncle 41–42 mm long; bracts deciduous, oblong; 4.7$\times$1.6 mm, apex obtuse, margin ciliate. \textbf{Staminate flowers}: pedicels 10.9 mm long.; perianth with 4 tepals, free, red, puverulent; the external ones size 12 $\times$ 5.5; ovate; obtuse; the internal ones 11.7 $\times$ 4.9; ovate; apex obtuse; stamens ca. 12; in a long column; obovate; conective not produced; styles multifid stygma linear. Horns slightly onward; tip apiculate

Distribution and habitat

frequent in hight elevations around the Medellin valley, but also recorded in most southern departments of Caldas and the west of Antioquia.

Taxonomic notes

Similar to the red-flowered \textit{Begonia urticae}, but characterized by have more elongate leaves, flowers separates in a branched staminate inflorescences and solitary pistilate flowers, that are bigger than in \textit{B. urticae}, and the staminal flowers have a longer staminal column.

Distribution and habitat

This species is endemic from the borders of the Sabana de Bogotá, the semi-flat region where the city is situated. It has been recorded in the western and south part of this region, growing in well conserved parch of forest.  

Taxonomic notes

This species was originally included in the iconography of the "Real Expedición Botánica" leaded by Jose Celestino Mutis. The species can be separated from \textit{Begonia guanentana} because the pubescence on the lower side of the leaf, and becaise the form of the fruits, wich have a long column and divergent horns in \textit{B. guanentana}, but have a short column and the horns are slightly backward orientated in \textit{B. cornuta}.
