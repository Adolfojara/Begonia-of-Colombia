Distribution and habiat

This species overlap partially its distribution with the close relative Begonia pax, growing in a northern region of the Serrania de los Yarigüies. It has been recorded from 1400 to 2200 m.

Taxonomic notes

 Begonia aguilarii is distinguished from B. pax Jara & Zabala by its glabrous stem and leaf undersides (vs. pubescent in B. pax), and its obovate to oblong (vs. elliptical) leaves. Leaves of B. aguilarii are also usually broader than the B. pax ones (1.6–5.6 vs. 0.9–1.8 cm).

Distribution and habitat

This species is endemic from the borders of the Sabana de Bogotá, the semi-flat region where the city is situated. It has been recorded in the western and south part of this region, growing conserved forest patches.  

Taxonomic notes

This species was originally included in the iconography of the "Real Expedición Botánica" leaded by Jose Celestino Mutis. The species can be separated from Begonia lawrencei because the pubescence on the lower side of the leaf, and because the form of the fruits, wich have a long column and divergent horns in B. lawrencei, but have a short column and the horns are slightly backward orientated in B. cornuta.
