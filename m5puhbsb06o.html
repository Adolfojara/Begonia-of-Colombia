Begonia antioquensis (A.DC.) Warb. Nat. Pflanzenfam. 3(6A): 146. A.DC. Casparya antioquiensis A.DC. Annales des Sciences Naturelles; Botanique, série 4 11: 116. 1859. TYPE: COLOMBIA. 1851-57. \textit{Triana s.n.} (holotype, P; isotype, K). 
Herb, 0.4 m. Branches pubescent or villous, stems and hairs reddish, hairs scale-like. Stipules sub-persistent, 6.8–8.1 mm long × 4.5–4.8 mm, ovate, pubescent, coriaceous, dark brown, obtuse, margin entire. Petiole 2.8–3.9 mm long., villous. Leaves mostly straight; 5.6–5.7 × 1.9–2 cm; oblog-elliptic, or lanceolate; strongly asymmetric; base rounded on one side, acute on the other side; apex acute; margin setose-serrate; above tuberculo-pilose; beneath tuberculo-pilose, or ferrugineo-tomentose on the veins; 5 veins pairs. Inflorescence bisexual. Male flowers distal and female flowers basal; protandrous, dichasial, 1–2 times divided; peduncle 41–42 mm long; bracts deciduous, oblong; 4.7 × 1.6 mm, apex obtuse, margin ciliate. Staminate flowers: pedicels 10.9 mm long.; perianth with 4 tepals, free, red, pulverulent; the external ones size 12 × 5.5; ovate; obtuse; the internal ones 11.7 × 4.9; ovate; apex obtuse; stamens ca. 12; in a long column; obovate; connective not produced; styles multifid stygma linear. Horns slightly onward; tip apiculate.

Distribution and habitat

frequent in high elevations around the Medellin valley, but also recorded in most southern departments of Caldas and the west of Antioquia.

Taxonomic notes

Similar to the red-flowered Begonia urticae, but characterized by have more elongate leaves, flowers separates in a branched staminate inflorescences and solitary pistilate flowers, that are bigger than in B. urticae, and the staminal flowers have a longer staminal column.

Distribution and habitat

Endemic from the Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta.

Taxonomic notes

This species can be easily recognized from other Casparya species from Colombia because the apex of the fruits is compressed horizontally, in contrast with most of the species where this is apiculate, or vertically compressed. Distribution and habitat

most of the collections of this species become from the Sibundoy valley in the department of Putumayo, there is however a record from the department of Cauca.

Taxonomic notes

The biz size of the male and female inflorescences, along with the short long of the fruit column are good characters to separate this species from almost glabrous and red flowered specimens of Begonia urticae.

Distribution and habitat

This species is endemic from the borders of the Sabana de Bogotá, the semi-flat region where the city is situated. It has been recorded in the western and south part of this region, growing conserved forest patches.  

Taxonomic notes

This species was originally included in the iconography of the "Real Expedición Botánica" leaded by Jose Celestino Mutis. The species can be separated from Begonia guanentana because the pubescence on the lower side of the leaf, and because the form of the fruits, wich have a long column and divergent horns in \textit{B. guanentana}, but have a short column and the horns are slightly backward orientated in \textit{B. cornuta}.
