Etymology

This species honors the colombian botanist Gloria Galeano (1958-2016), who was a specialist in taxonomy and systematics of plants, mainly of the families Arecaceae and Cyclantaceae, and also studied the flora of the Choco region. In his thirty years in the Instituto de Ciencias Naturales Dr. Galeano collected hundreds of specimens, included ones of the first collections of the species here described.

Distribution adn habitat

This species is restricted to the eastern basin of the Eastern Colombian cordillera, and have been collected in two disjunct localities (figure XX), one in the Department of Boyaca and the other one in the Department of Cundinamarca. The zones where the species has been collected is cloud forest, from ca. 1700 to 2500 m, in shadow zones of well conserved zones, and close to rainfalls.

Distribution and habitat

 Begonia guanentana is endemic to the cloud forest  of the Colombian Eastern Cordillera, with a narrow distribution restricted to the locality of Virolín and contiguous vicinities, between 1700 and 2500 m. The forest of this region is dominated by Quercus humboldtii (Fagaceae), accompanied by highly diverse families such as Rubiaceae, Melastomataceae and Lauraceae in genera such as Miconia, Anthurium and Psychotria  \cite{Reina_2004}. In the region there has been recorded several biogeographicaly interesting species, such as the monotypic Colombobalanus excelsa (Lozano, Hern. Cam. & Henao, Jesús Eugenio) Nixon & Crepet, only known from three other localities in Colombia \cite{Aguirre_Acosta_2018}, as well as the endemic species: Arthrostylidium virolinense Londoño & L.G. Clark, Eugenia fernandez-alonsoi Parra-Os., and Magnolia virolinensis (Lozano) Govaerts.

Taxonomic notes

Specimens of this species have been frequently misplaced in herbaria as Begonia cornuta, however, both species can be separate as indicated in the diagnosis. Specimens from the southern part of the distribution range, in the department of Boyacá are slightly different, usually these specimens have bigger inflorescences, with more persistent bracts. However, variation is not great enough to separate this morphotype as a different species or variety.

Conservation status

Due to the extent of occurrence (43000 km2) the species is critically endangered (CR), and according to the area of occupancy (28000 km2) the species is endangered (EN). The region where the species grows has a moderate intervention of agriculture and pasture, however the region includes a protected area, the Santuario de Flora y Fauna de Iguaque, which recently was extended to the Cuchilla del Fara.

Refers to the Guanentá province, in the Department of Santander, where most of the records of this species come from.
