Etymology

Refers to the name of the mountain to which this species is endemic, the Cerro Mamapacha in the Colombian department of Boyacá, shared by the municipalities of Garagoa and Chinavita.  

Habitat and distribution

Begonia mamapachensis is endemic to the mountains known as Cerro Mamapacha, in a range between 2100 and 2500 m. Plants of the species has been found growing in the understory of the cloud forest, usually along tracks and stream borders.

Proposed IUCN conservation status

Because the extent of occurrence (43000 km2) the species is critically endangered (CR), and according to the area of occupancy (28000 km2) it is endangered (EN). The region where the species grows has a moderate intervention of agriculture and pasture, and is not part of any national or regional protected area, however, there are private reserves in the zone of Cerro Mamapacha. The plant is cultivated now in the special collection for conservation (CEPAC) at the Bogotá Botanic Garden.

Bracts caducous, 7.1–8.5 × 4.4–4.8 mm, oblong; those on the base of the inflorescence usually with apex acute or obtuse, the secondary ones with the apex rounded or truncate; margin pilose. Staminate flowers: pedicels 7.2–27.5 mm long; two perianth series, outer tepals 2, free, oblong to sub-orbicular, 13.9–14.5 × 11.8–13.2 mm, rounded to the apex, white and usually reddish at the abaxial side, with glandular, and non-glandular simple and bifid hairs on the abaxial side, internal tepals two, free, elliptic to linear-lanceolate, the apex acute to obtuse, 9.4–11.2 × 3.6–4.3 mm, white, glabrous; stamens 19–23, held on a short column 0.9–1.2 mm long, filaments 0.8–1.6 mm, anthers oblong, 4.0–4.2 × 1.4–1.6 mm; connective scarcely pronounced 0.5–0.7 mm long. Pistillate flowers: bracteoles absent; pedicels 3.9–6.7 mm; perianth with five free tepals, three internal and two outer; outer tepals elliptic to obovate, 21.0–21.4 × 9.5–10.1 mm, glabrous, white, margin entire, apex acute obtuse to rounded; internal tepals 17.3–21.4 × 7.5–8.8 mm; styles multifid, 2.3–6.5 mm long; stigmatic surface covering the apex of the styles. Ovary turbinate; placentae simple with ovules covering all the surface. Fruits: capsule turbinate, trilocular, with three lateral “horns”, pubescent, with glandular and non-glandular hairs, 19.2–30.3 × 18.8–26.3 mm, horns 6.2–11.1 mm, column 11.2–18.4 × 1.8–4.9 mm.  
Habitat and distribution
In the north of the Colombian Eastern Cordillera in the deparment of Santander, and in the Venezuelan states Táchira and Mérida. The Colombian population have the leaves shorter and the margin more deeply lobulate.
