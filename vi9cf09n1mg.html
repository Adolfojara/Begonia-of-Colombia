26. Begonia trianae (A. DC.) Warb. (1894: 146). Casparya trianae A. DC. (1859: 117). Type:–COLOMBIA. Provincia de Bogotá, Ubalá, 1750 m, 1855, J.J. Triana 5048(5153) (holotype BM!, isotype COL!).
Herb to subffrutex, to 1 m. Branches more or less covered with ferrugineous crispo-pilose pubescence. Stipules sub-persistent, 8–10 mm long, ovate or auriculate, coriaceous, green, obtuse. Petiole 12–32 mm long. Foliar lamina transverse; 2–5; 3–10 cm; ovate; strongly asymmetryc; base rounded on one side, auriculate on the other side; apex acute; margin crenate-serrulate. Infloresence unisexual in monoecious plants. Male inflorescence dichasial; peduncle 2.5–6 cm long. Perianth with 4 tepals; free; white; glabrous to pilose; the external ones size 6–8 mm wide.; suborvicular; the internal ones smaller; narrowly obovate; oblong; conective few produced, obtuse. Female inflorescence solitary flowers; bracts present; deciduous; 8 mm long.; ovate; margin ciliate-serrulate; pilose. Tepals 5 tepals; free; 6–8 mm long; obovate; apex acute; similar to the outers; pubescent; styles multifid; Stygma twisted. Horns slightly onward; tip flattened in a vertical plane.

Habitat and distribution

Endemic to the eastern basin of the Eastern Colombian Cordillera, in the department of Cundinamarca, between 1750 and 2500 m. 

Taxonomic notes

The specimen Schlim 1145 cited by Smith & Shubert (1946), probaby correspond to a different species from Norte de Santander, because the different form of the fruit.
