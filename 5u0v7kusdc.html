Taxonomic treatment

Begonia aguilarii Jara & Zabala-Rivera. Novon 26 (4): 355–363. TYPE: Colombia: Santander: Mpio. El Hato, vereda Hoya Negra, predio Golcondas [Golconda], quebrada La Vega, Parque Nacional Natural Serranía de los Yariguíes y su zona de amortiguamiento, 6º36'27.8"N 73º21'33.7"W, 2000 m, 20 Feb 2010.  Aguilar-Cano 516 (hotype, FMB!; isotype, UIS!).
Erect herb, 0.4–1 m, Branches glabrous, without lenticels. Stipules early deciduous, oblong or ovate, glabrous, membranaceous, hialine, obtuse, margin entire. Petiole 1.1–6.9 (8.1) mm long. glabrous. Leaves mostly straight, 3.2–10.6 × 1.6–5.6 cm, elliptic or obovate; asymetric; base rounded on one side, obtuse on the other side, apex acuminate, margin doubly serrate; above tuberculo-pilose, with the hairs disperse between the secondary veins; beneath glabrous; 5–7 veins pairs. Infloresence unisexual in monoecious plants. Male inflorescence: cincinum (umbell-like); 1–3 flowers; peduncle 0.37–0.43 cm long.; bracts present, perstent, 6.2–7.4 × 5.7–6.2 mm., Obovate, or oblong, apex obtuse, margin entire, glabrous. Male flowers: perianth with 4 tepals; free; white; glabrous; the external ones size 4.9–7.1 × (2.6–)3.1–3.3(–4); ovate; obtuse; the internal ones (3.7–)5.2–6.5 × 1.0–1.2 (–2.2); elliptic; apex acute, or obtuse; stamens 8–9; in a short column; filaments 0.1–0.2 mm long.; anthers (2.5–)3.1–3.6 × 0.5–0.6 (–1.3); ovoid; conective not produced. Female inflorescence solitary flowers; bracts absent. Female flowers: Pedicel 5.6–5.9 mm; bracteoles absent; persisitent; 6.2–7.4 × 5.2–5.7; ovate; apex obtuse; margin entire; glabrous; tepals 5, free; 9.2–10.2 × 4.8–5.0; obovate; apex obtuse; margin entire. (3.7–)5.2–6.5 × 1.0–1.2 (–2.2), linear-obovate; apex obtuse; glabrous; ovary glabrous; styles 4–5; 7.9–8.2 mm long.; Stygma linear. Fruit 13.4–13.9 × 22.9–31.7; glabrous; column 0 mm; horns perpendicular respect to the central axis; tip flattened in a vertical plane.

Distribution and habitat

This species overlap partially its distribution with the close relative B. pax, growing in a northern region of the Yariquies serrania. It has been recorded from 1400 to 2200 m.

Taxonomic notes

This is a species close related with other species from the north of the eastern cordillera. From \textit{Begonia pax} this can be distinguished because the glabrous lamina below.
