1. Begonia aguilarii Jara.  Novon 26:356. figs.1-3. (2018). TYPE. COLOMBIA. Santander: Mpio. El Hato, vereda Hoya Negra, predio Golcondas [Golconda], quebrada La Vega, Parque Nacional Natural Serranía de los Yariguíes y su zona de amortiguamiento, 6º36'27.8"N 73º21'33.7"W, 2000 m, 20 Feb 2010, J. Aguilar-Cano 516 (holotype, FMB, isotype, UIS).   
Erect herb, 0.4–1 m. Branches glabrous, without lenticels. Stipules early deciduous, oblong or ovate, glabrous, membranaceous, hialine, obtuse, margin entire. Petiole 1.1–6.9 mm long. (8.1), glabrous. Leaves mostly straight, 3.2–10.6 × 1.6–5.6 cm, elliptic, or obovate, asymmetric; base rounded on one side, obtuse on the other side;, apex acuminate, margin doubly serrate, above tuberculo-pilose, with the hais disperse between the secondary veins, beneath glabrous, 5–7 veins pairs. Infloresence unisexual in monoecious plants. Male inflorescence cincinum (umbell-like), 1–3 flowers; peduncle 0.37–0.43 cm long, bracts present; persistent; 6.2–7.4 mm. 5.7–6.2 mm. Obovate, or oblong; apex obtuse; margin entire; glabrous. Perianth with 2 tepals; free; white; glabrous; the external ones size 4.9–7.1 x (2.6–)3.1–3.3(–4); ovate; obtuse; the internal ones (3.7–)5.2–6.5 3 1.0–1.2 (–2.2); elliptic; apex acute, or obtuse; stamens 8–9; in a short column; filaments 0.1–0.2 mm long.; anthers (2.5–)3.1–3.6 x 0.5–0.6 (–1.3); ovoid; conective not produced. Female inflorescence solitary flowers; bracts absent. Pistillate Flowers: Pedicel 5.6–5.9 mm; bracteoles absent; persisitent; 6.2–7.4 x 5.2–5.7; ovate; apex obtuse; margin entire; glabrous; tepals 5 tepals; free; 9.2–10.2 x 4.8–5.0; obovate; apex obtuse; margin entire. (3.7–)5.2–6.5 x 1.0–1.2 (–2.2). Linear-obovate; apex obtuse; glabrous; ovary glabrous; styles 4–5; 7.9–8.2 mm long.; Stygma linear. Fruit 13.4–13.9 x 22.9–31.7; glabrous; column 0 mm; horns perpendicular respect to the central axis; tip flattened in a vertical plane.
