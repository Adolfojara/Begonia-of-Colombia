Begonia solaniflora Jara. 2017. Phytotaxa 321(2): 209. Colombia: Santander, Suaita, corregimiento San José de Suaita, entre Marbella y La Meseta. 6.254°N, 73.4231°W, 1640 m, A. Jara & J. Luna 2568, 17 Dic 2013 (Holotype: ANDES!; Isotype: COL!).
Herb, to 0.7 m. Branches glabrous, todes thickened and usually reddish, up to 2.5 mm across, with internodes 6.7–31.3 (65.4) mm long. Stipules early deciduous, 9.7–10.6 mm long, 2.3–3.8 mm, elliptic-lanceolate, glabrous, membranaceous, hialine or pale greenish, obtuse or acute, margin entire. Petiole 3.3–10.2 mm long.; glabrous (with some bristles near the junction with the blade). Foliar lamina mostly straight; 4.1–8.5 cm; 1.4–2.5 cm; ovate, or elliptic; slightly asymmetryc; base cuneate; apex acute; margin serrate; with a hair between each pair of teeth; above tuberculo-pilose, or adpresed pubescent.
Begonia suaviola Jara (XX, 2016). Type:—COLOMBIA. Valle del Cauca: municipio de El Cairo, vereda El Brillante, reserva natural Cerro El Inglés, 2217–2328 m, 27 September 2013, Jara, A. & Avila, F. 2447 (holotype ANDES!, isotypes CUVC!, HUA!, E! (to be distributed)).
Herb. Branches puberulous. Stipules persistent, 4.6–13 mm long, 2–4.6 mm, oblong to ovate, puberulous, coriaceous, pale greenish to hialine, acute (with a seta of ca. 1 mm long.), margin entire. Petiole 1.1–9.1 mm long. Foliar lamina mostly straight; 3.4–8.7 cm; 0.9–3.1 cm; oblong to elliptic; slightly asymmetryc; base oblique by one side and auriculate on the othes side; apex acuminate; margin doubly serrate and ciliate; above densely hirtellous; beneath villose; 6–10 veins pairs. Infloresence bisexual and male; male flowers basal and female flowers distal; protandrous; cincinum; peduncle 15.5–38.4 mm long; bracts deciduous; 3.8–11.1 mm long.; 0.8–5.3 mm; apex acute; margin entire.
Habitat and distribution:— on the border between the Colombian departments of Valle del Cauca and Chocó, in cloud forest, between 1700–2500 m.
