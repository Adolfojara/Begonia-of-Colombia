6. Begonia diffusa L.B. Sm. & B.G. Schub. Caldasia 4(16): 32–33, t. 6. 1946. TYPE. COLOMBIA. Santander: Quebrada de Pais (Paez?), north of La Baja, ca. 3200 m, E.P. Killip & A.C. Smith 18772 (holotype, NY; isotypes, GH, US).
Caulescent herb, to 30 cm high. Stem prostate; Branches more or less covered with ferrugineous crispo-pilose pubescence. Stipules persistent, oblong, 2.7–4.4 × 0.8–2 mm, apex acute, membranaceous, maroon, glabrous, margin entire. Petiole 3–18 mm long; blade asymmetric, elliptic, 7–24 × 5–13.3 mm, membranous, apex obtuse, base oblique, margin dentate, with a terminal seta on each teeth of ca. 0.7 mm, upper surface pilose, lowers surfa pilose on veins, veins palmate-pinnate,  3–4 pairs. Inflorescences unisexual, the male one with ca. 4 flowers, peduncle 19.9–29.8 mm long, sparsely pilose, bracts persistent, oblong, ca. 2.2  ×  1.2 mm, maroon, glabrous, apex obtuse, margin entire, apex ciliate; female inflorescence solitary, peduncle pilose, bracts not seen. Male flowers: pedicels to 8 mm long, glabrous; tepals 4, spreading, outer 2 ovate, ca. 4–6  ×  2–3 mm, apex obtuse, base cordate, white and pink-tinged, pilose on the outside, margin entire, inner 2 oblong, ca. 4.2 × 2.2 mm, apex rounded, base acute, white, glabrous, margin entire; stamens numerous, yellow, ca. 1 mm long, on a short torous, 1.6 × 0.6 mm long, connective produced ca. 0.5 mm. Female flowers: pedicels ca. 1 mm long, bracteoles not seen; tepals 5, subequal, spreading, elliptic, 9–14 × 2–5mm, apex rounded or obtuse, glabrous, margin entire, white; styles 3, multifid, ca. 3 mm long. Fruit turbinate, 8.8–12.7 × 10–15.1 mm, horns with apex apiculate, forward oriented, column 3.2 –5.8 mm long.          
 

Distribution and habitat

Only known from from the high mountains in the paramo of Santurbán, in the department of Santander.

Taxonomic notes

Similar to B. urticae, but differentiated from this species because the grown form and the lobulated leaves.....
