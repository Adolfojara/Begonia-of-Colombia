Begonia diffusa L.B. Sm. & B.G. Schub. Caldasia 4(16): 32–33, t. 6. 1946. TYPE. COLOMBIA. \textbf{Santander}: Quebrada de Pais (Paez?), north of La Baja, ca. 3200 m, \textit{E.P. Killip & A.C. Smith 18772} (holotype, NY; isotypes, GH, US).
Herb ca. 0.3. Branches more or less covered with ferrugineous crispo-pilose pubescence. \textbf{Stipules} sub-persistent, 4–5 mm long, oblong, membranaceous, setulose, margin entire. Petiole 3–18 mm long. \textbf{Leaves} mostly straight; 7–20; 18–34 cm; elliptic; slightly asymmetryc; base dimidiate; margin setose-serrate. Perianth with 4 tepals. Horns slightly onward.

Distribution and habitat

Only known from from the high mountains in the paramo of Santurbán, in the department of Santander.

Taxonomic notes

Similar to \textit{B. urticae}, but differentiated from this species because the grown form and the lobulated leaves.....

Habitat and distribution:— This species is restricted to the eastern basin of the Eastern Colombian cordillera, and have been collected in two disjunct localities (figure XX), one in the Department of Boyaca and the other one in the Department of Cundinamarca. The zones where the species has been collected is cloud forest, from ca. 1700 to 2500 m, in shadow zones of well conserved zones, and close to rainfalls.
